Content notes

This is being used in the Access to Work service.

It was designed to overcome the difficulty faced by some people in giving appropriate contact details. For example, deaf applicants who use an interpreter found it unclear whether they should nominate them, and whether to give their own or their interpreter's contact details.

This content was proven to let people name formal or informal (ie, legal) representatives and give the most appropriate contact details for their needs.

Note, content should be specific to the service about the things that the representative will be asked about, you don't have to use the things listed in the example.

Update: August 2016

The security of this pattern has been challenged by Access to Work operations on the grounds that it is enables fraud should a supplier represent a user.

Although such fraud has happened in the past, it requires collusion between supplier, employer and applicant throughout the process, including assessment. Therefore it isn't clear why this pattern is more or less robust than any other.

In fact an advantage of digital in this scenario is that data could be employed to identify if a company or indvidual is nominated many times in similar cases. Previous fraud has been only identified by coincidence when an individual adviser recognised a representative as a supplier.

A workshop is planned in September to explore this issue.
