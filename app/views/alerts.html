Inset text

Bordered text is used to draw attention to important content on the page. This can appear within the flow of body content. This element is detailed in the Government Service Design Manual.

It takes up to 8 weeks to register a lasting power of attorney.

Alert message

Alert messages usually appear at the top of the page, underneath the main heading. They span the width of a text column. In full-width layout keep the maximum number of characters per line to about 75 (â…” the width of the page at desktop size).

Default alert message

Use this when:

  • a section of text is the most important feature of the page
  • when text needs to be highlighted and inset text hasn't worked
  • the user must take a significant action apart from the service, eg make a phone call
Contact Jobcentre Plus to find out how much you owe the Social Fund.
{# /example #}

If you haven’t booked your Universal Credit interview, call 035 600 4272 or 0845 600 4272 to arrange a date and time.

Open hours are 8am to 6pm, Monday to Friday.

{# /example #}
Register within 7 days or you’ll have to register again.

Positive alert message

Use this when:

  • there's been a discrete action during a transaction, but the user hasn’t completed their full journey, eg they've sent something or saved something

Application saved.

Legal text

You must call out anything that may affect the user personally or financially. This element is detailed in the Government Service Design Manual.

A border is not required around and it can be included anywhere within the main content of a page. Almost all transactional services will have this on a declaration page, warning of consequences of fraud.

{#

Legal text - icon alignment centre

Use this when:

  • messages are short and up to around 75 characters (one line at wide browser viewports)
#}
Warning You can be fined up to £5,000 if you don’t register.
{# /example #}

Use a vertically centred icon when messages are short and up to around 75 characters (1 line at wide browser viewports)

Warning

You must repay any money you're asked to.

If you don't repay money when asked you could be taken to court, and your benefits reduced or money taken directly from your wages until your debt is repaid.

{# /example #}

Use a top-aligned icon when messages are longer than 75 characters (2 lines or more at wide browser viewports)

Error message

This element is detailed in the Government Service Design Manual and normally appears above the main heading of a page.

Use this when:

  • the user has done something that's caused an error
  • there’s a problem that the user needs to do something about

There’s a problem

Check your form. You must:

  • enter your full name
  • enter a valid email address
{# /example #}

Your partner hasn’t created their account.

They must:

  1. go to the homepage
  2. select ‘Register with a partner code’
  3. enter this code: YF92NEXT

They have 7 days left - this code won't work after 26 June 2016.

If they don't register in time you'll both have to register again.

{# /example #}

Confirmation message

Use this when:

  • a user has completed an action successfully
  • a user has finished a transaction

Your application has been received
